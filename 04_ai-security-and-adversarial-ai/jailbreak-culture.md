# Jailbreak culture

**Type:** AI Security culture / adversarial prompting / community signal

## Why it is on my radar

I became interested in jailbreak culture through AI red team discussions, Pliny the Liberator, BAUSI, Jason Haddix interviews and DEF CON-adjacent conversations around model safety and adversarial testing.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is not the prompts themselves. It is the speed at which communities test new models, share claims, build status around bypasses and challenge vendor safety claims.

It feels similar to older hacker subcultures: part research, part play, part clout, part real security signal.

## What I take from it

Jailbreak culture helped me understand that AI Security is also an ecosystem problem. Defenders need to observe the communities, the narratives and the claimed techniques without blindly amplifying unsafe content.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), the challenge is separating hype from signal: is this just a refusal bypass, or does it expose data, tools, agents or real workflows?

## Current limit

I follow the ecosystem, but I do not want to reproduce jailbreak techniques or make the repo look like a misuse guide.

## Resources I actually used

Pliny-related interviews, Jason Haddix discussions, BAUSI/Discord observation, AI red teaming material and public AI Security research.
