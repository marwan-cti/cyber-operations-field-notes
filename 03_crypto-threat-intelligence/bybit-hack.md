# Bybit hack

**Type:** crypto exchange hack / signing workflow / DPRK attribution

## Why it is on my radar

Bybit caught my attention because it is one of the most significant recent crypto hacks and because public attribution linked it to North Korean cyber actors. It sits exactly at the intersection of crypto, Lazarus-style operations, wallet security and incident response.

## What stuck with me

The most interesting part for me is not only the amount stolen. It is the signing workflow problem: the idea that signers may believe they are approving something legitimate while the underlying transaction logic is malicious.

That makes the case more subtle than “private keys were stolen”. It is about trust in interfaces, third-party wallet infrastructure, transaction verification and operational habits.

## What I take from it

Bybit helped me understand that major crypto theft often abuses trust before it abuses cryptography. The final on-chain transfer is visible, but the important CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence) question is what failed before that: identity, workflow, signing, vendor trust, social engineering or verification.

## Current limit

Because the case is recent and heavily covered, I need to be careful not to treat every public thread as confirmed fact.
