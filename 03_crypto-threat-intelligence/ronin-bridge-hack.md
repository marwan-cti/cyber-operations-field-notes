# Ronin Bridge hack

**Type:** crypto hack / bridge compromise / Lazarus-linked theft

## Why it is on my radar

I discovered Ronin while digging into major crypto hacks and Lazarus-linked operations. It kept coming up as one of the biggest bridge hacks and as a key case connecting crypto, North Korea, social engineering and on-chain laundering.

## What stuck with me

What marked me is that Ronin was not only a “crypto exploit” story. It showed that bridge security also depends on validators, access control, social engineering, operational shortcuts and trust assumptions.

The case also made Lazarus feel concrete to me: not as an abstract APT (Advanced Persistent Threat) label, but as an actor linked to real theft, laundering and strategic financing.

## What I take from it

Ronin helped me understand why bridges are high-value targets. They concentrate trust and liquidity. If attackers can abuse the validator model or signing process, the impact can be massive.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), Ronin connects technical compromise, actor attribution, blockchain tracing, sanctions and geopolitical context.
