# ZachXBT and on-chain investigations

**Type:** crypto OSINT / on-chain investigation / independent researcher

## Why it is on my radar

I discovered ZachXBT through crypto hack investigations and public threads around scams, stolen funds and laundering. His work stood out because it showed a different kind of investigator: independent, pseudonymous, fast-moving and very connected to the crypto ecosystem.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is the mix between on-chain tracing and OSINT (Open Source Intelligence). Wallet movements are public, but the interpretation is not automatic. ZachXBT’s work shows how investigators connect addresses, transactions, exchanges, social media, victim reports and scam infrastructure.

## What I take from it

ZachXBT helped me understand that crypto investigations are evidence chains. A wallet address is not an identity. A transaction is not an attribution by itself. The value comes from connecting pieces carefully and showing why a conclusion is plausible.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), this is useful because it trains source evaluation and attribution caution.

## Current limit

I follow the logic of on-chain investigation, but I still need deeper hands-on practice with blockchain analytics tools.

## Resources I actually used

ZachXBT public investigations, crypto Twitter/X discussions, reporting around Bybit and other hacks, and institutional reports from blockchain intelligence firms for comparison.
