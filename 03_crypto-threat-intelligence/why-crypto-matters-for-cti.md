# Why crypto matters for CTI

**Type:** domain note / crypto / threat intelligence

## Why it is on my radar

Crypto is part of my background and part of my cyber culture. Before focusing seriously on cybersecurity, I had already spent time around crypto, web3, tokens and NFT projects. Later, when I started digging into CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), I realized that crypto was also a major threat intelligence domain.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is that crypto hacks sit between technical compromise, financial crime, OSINT (Open Source Intelligence), laundering, sanctions and geopolitics.

Unlike many other forms of cybercrime, part of the money trail can be visible on-chain. But visibility does not mean easy attribution. You still need context, off-chain evidence and caution.

## What I take from it

Crypto threat intelligence helps me connect several things I care about: hacks, social engineering, Lazarus, ZachXBT, wallet tracing, exchanges, bridges, laundering and public-private response.

It also shows why CTI is not only about malware. Sometimes the most important part of the story is what happens after the compromise: where the money goes, who helps freeze it, how it is laundered, and what that says about the actor.

## Current limit

I understand the ecosystem and major cases better than the deep technical details of smart contracts and blockchain analytics tooling.

## Resources I actually used

ZachXBT investigations, crypto hack reporting, Chainalysis/TRM/Elliptic-style references, FBI/Treasury statements, and cases like Ronin, Bybit and Lazarus-linked thefts.
