# Florian Roth and YARA

**Type:** detection engineering / YARA / Sigma / threat hunting

## Why it is on my radar

I discovered Florian Roth through YARA, Sigma, detection engineering references and his DEF CON content. His work stood out because it connects threat knowledge with practical detection.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is the idea that a detection rule is not just syntax. It is analyst judgment written into a format that tools can use.

His DEF CON material made this more concrete for me: detection is a living craft, not just copying public rules from GitHub.

That matters because I do not want CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence) to stay purely narrative. I want to understand how a report, a malware family or an actor profile can become a hunting idea or a detection rule.

## What I take from it

Florian Roth’s work helped me see detection as a craft: hypotheses, false positives, telemetry, maintainability and context.

It also connects well with my transition path because SOC (Security Operations Center), threat hunting and CTI all meet around the question: “what can we actually detect from what we know?”

## Current limit

I still need to practice writing and testing YARA/Sigma rules myself instead of only understanding their role conceptually.

## Resources I actually used

Florian Roth / Neo23x0 GitHub, SigmaHQ, YARA references, his DEF CON content, security community discussions and detection engineering material.
