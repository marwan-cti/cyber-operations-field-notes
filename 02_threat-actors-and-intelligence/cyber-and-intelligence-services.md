# Cyber and intelligence services

**Type:** strategic context / cyber operations / intelligence

## Why it is on my radar

A lot of the cyber stories that marked me are linked, directly or indirectly, to intelligence services: Stuxnet, Shadow Brokers, Equation Group reporting, GRU-linked APTs, Lazarus and some supply chain or espionage cases.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is the way cyber can serve missions that go beyond “hacking”. It can support espionage, sabotage, influence, preparation of the environment, strategic pressure or financing.

This is also where I need to be careful. The topic can quickly become sensationalist if I talk about services, units or elite teams without enough public evidence.

## What I take from it

For my CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence) culture, the key lesson is that cyber operations must be read in layers: technical artifacts, target choice, timing, actor behavior, public attribution and geopolitical context.

I do not want to sound like I have secret knowledge. I want to show that I understand why intelligence context matters, while keeping attribution cautious.

## Current limit

I need to keep separating what is publicly documented from what is speculative or based on narratives around intelligence services.

## Resources I actually used

Cyber history videos, MITRE ATT&CK, vendor reports, public reporting around Stuxnet, Shadow Brokers, APT28/Sandworm, Lazarus and investigations around Russian intelligence-linked operations.
