# NSO Group and Pegasus

**Type:** spyware / private surveillance industry / mobile exploitation

## Why it is on my radar

After Stuxnet and Unit 8200, I became interested in the wider Israeli cyber landscape, including private companies selling offensive surveillance capabilities. Pegasus and NSO Group are central to that topic.

## What stuck with me

What marked me is the shift from state cyber operations to commercialized surveillance tools. Pegasus showed me that very advanced mobile exploitation can become a product sold to government clients.

It also raises a difficult tension: these tools are presented as useful against terrorism and serious crime, but public investigations have linked Pegasus to the targeting of journalists, activists, dissidents and political figures.

## What I take from it

NSO/Pegasus helped me understand that cyber capability is also an export market, not only a state secret.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), this matters because the attacker is not always a classic APT group. Sometimes the ecosystem includes vendors, clients, exploit brokers, mobile zero-days, forensic traces and legal/political controversy.

## Current limit

I need to study the technical side of Pegasus more deeply, especially mobile exploitation chains and forensic detection, without reducing the topic to scandal or ideology.

## Resources I actually used

Public reporting on Pegasus, NSO Group, Forbidden Stories / Amnesty-style investigations, Citizen Lab references, articles on mobile zero-click exploitation and discussions about the private spyware industry.
