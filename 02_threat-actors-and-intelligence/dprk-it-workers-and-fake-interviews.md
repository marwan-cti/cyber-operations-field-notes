# DPRK IT workers and fake interviews

**Type:** social engineering / insider risk / DPRK revenue generation

## Why it is on my radar

I dug into this topic while researching Lazarus, crypto hacks and North Korean cyber operations. What caught my attention was the overlap between fake interviews, remote IT jobs, crypto companies, deepfake-enabled identities and laptop farms used to make North Korean workers appear to be located in the US or other countries.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is that this is not a classic malware-only story. It is social engineering, HR process abuse, identity fraud, remote work, insider risk and state-linked revenue generation at the same time.

The fake interview angle also feels very modern: fabricated profiles, remote hiring, AI-assisted communication, deepfake or synthetic identity signals, and companies that may not realize the person they hired is not who they claim to be.

## What I take from it

This topic helped me understand that DPRK-linked cyber operations are not limited to hacks like Ronin or Bybit. The same strategic objective — generating revenue and gaining access — can appear through employment fraud and trusted access.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), it connects actor tracking with HR security, identity verification, endpoint monitoring, contractor risk and social engineering.

## Current limit

I understand the pattern and why it matters, but I still need to study more real case reports and detection guidance around laptop farms, remote-worker verification and deepfake interview signals.
