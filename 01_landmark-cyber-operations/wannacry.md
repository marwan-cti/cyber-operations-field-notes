# WannaCry

**Type:** ransomware worm / exploit leak / global incident

## Why it is on my radar

I discovered WannaCry through YouTube videos, Wikipedia, MITRE ATT&CK and interviews around Marcus Hutchins, the researcher who found the kill switch. I also kept seeing the same names around the story: EternalBlue, MS17-010 and Shadow Brokers.

## What stuck with me

What struck me is that WannaCry was not just “a ransomware”. It was a global incident made possible by a combination of leaked offensive tooling, systems that were not patched, exposed legacy protocols and worm-like propagation.

The Marcus Hutchins kill switch story also stayed with me because it shows the impact one skilled researcher can have during a live incident, while also reminding me that slowing propagation is not the same as fixing the damage.

## What I take from it

WannaCry helped me understand that a vulnerability becomes much more urgent when reliable exploit code is public and easy to reuse. It also made the Shadow Brokers story feel concrete: leaked tools do not stay inside their original context.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), the case connects vulnerability intelligence, exploit availability, patch management and operational impact.

## Current limit

I understand the strategic and operational lesson better than the low-level SMB exploitation details.
