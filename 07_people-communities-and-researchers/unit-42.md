# Unit 42

**Type:** vendor research team / threat intelligence / incident response

## Why it is on my radar

I came across Unit 42 through threat intelligence reports, cyber articles and references shared by the security community. It is one of the vendor teams that helped me understand what professional CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence) production looks like.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is the bridge between research and action. A good Unit 42-style report is not just a list of indicators. It connects campaign context, malware, actor behavior, impact and what defenders can actually do.

It also reminds me that vendor research is valuable but not neutral in a perfect sense: every vendor has telemetry, product and visibility biases.

## What I take from it

Unit 42 helps me understand the standard I should aim for when reading or producing CTI: clear context, evidence, confidence, defensive relevance and practical recommendations.

## Current limit

I have read references and reports, but I still need to study more Unit 42 reports deeply and compare them with Mandiant, Microsoft, ESET, Kaspersky and CrowdStrike research.

## Resources I actually used

Unit 42 blog posts and reports, references in cyber research, vendor-report comparisons and CTI discussions.
