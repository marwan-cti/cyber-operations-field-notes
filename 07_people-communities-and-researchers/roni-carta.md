# Roni Carta

## Status

Draft v1 — person / ecosystem profile.

## How I discovered him

I discovered Roni Carta through interviews, conferences and public talks. What caught my attention was not only the technical level, but also the visible passion, curiosity and creative energy behind his work.

I follow his work on LinkedIn and through articles related to Lupin & Holmes and Depi, especially around software supply chain attacks, dependency confusion and upstream security.

## Why he matters in my cyber culture

Roni Carta matters to me because he represents a type of hacker profile I find especially interesting: offensive creativity used in a legal, research-driven and defensive direction.

His public work around supply chain vulnerabilities shows that major security failures are not always at the obvious front door. They can appear upstream: dependencies, package registries, build systems, maintainer accounts, forgotten domains, CI/CD workflows and trusted developer infrastructure.

This is directly relevant to how I want to think as a future CTI analyst: not only asking “what vulnerability exists?”, but “what path would a creative attacker actually take?”

## Area

- Bug bounty and ethical hacking.
- Software supply chain security.
- Dependency confusion.
- Maintainer and account takeover risk.
- Upstream attack paths.
- AppSec (Application Security) and CTI-adjacent thinking.

## What I learned from following his work

- Supply chain risk is often hidden in trusted developer workflows.
- Dependencies are not only code; they are also maintainers, accounts, domains, registries and update paths.
- A single weak link can affect many downstream organizations.
- Good offensive research can be translated into defensive product thinking.
- Passion and curiosity matter, but they need ethical boundaries and responsible disclosure.

## Relevance for CTI

For CTI, Roni Carta's work is useful because it pushes analysis beyond classic IOC (Indicator of Compromise) collection. Supply chain threats require understanding relationships: which package depends on which maintainer, which build pipeline trusts which component, and which organization would be exposed if an upstream account were compromised.

This type of thinking helps connect threat modeling, AppSec, vulnerability management and intelligence prioritization.

## Caveats

I should avoid turning this profile into hero worship. The useful lesson is not “Roni is a genius”, even if his work is impressive. The useful lesson is how his research shows a mindset: creative offensive reasoning, responsible disclosure and focus on real attack paths.

I should also distinguish between public claims, company messaging, technical blog posts and independently verifiable technical evidence.
