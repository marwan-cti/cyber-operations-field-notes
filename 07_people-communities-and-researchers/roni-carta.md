# Roni Carta

**Type:** supply chain security / bug bounty / offensive research

## Why he is on my radar

I discovered Roni Carta through interviews, conferences and public talks, then started following his work on LinkedIn and through Lupin & Holmes / Depi articles.

## What stuck with me

What I liked immediately was the mix of passion, technical creativity and clear obsession with real attack paths. His work around supply chain risk made me understand that a vulnerability is not always in the obvious place.

Sometimes the weak point is upstream: a dependency, a package, a domain, a maintainer account, a CI/CD path or a forgotten trust relationship.

## What I take from it

Roni Carta represents the kind of hacker mindset I find inspiring: offensive creativity, but used in a legal and defensive direction.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), his work pushes me to ask a better question than “where is the bug?”: what path would a creative attacker actually take?

## Current limit

I still need to practice supply chain testing more concretely instead of mostly understanding it through articles and interviews.

## Resources I actually used

Tronche de Tech interviews, Roni Carta’s LinkedIn posts, Lupin & Holmes / Depi articles and supply chain security discussions.
