# Pliny the Liberator

**Type:** AI jailbreak culture / adversarial AI persona

## Why he is on my radar

I first heard Pliny mentioned in a DEF CON-related context, then again through Jason Haddix interviews and AI red team discussions.

## What stuck with me

What interests me is not just the technical side of jailbreaks, but the cultural side: personas, communities, narratives and the speed at which people test new models.

Pliny is useful to me as a signal of the adversarial AI ecosystem, not as a source of techniques to reproduce.

## What I take from it

Pliny helped me understand that AI Security is not only about model providers and papers. There are also public communities pushing boundaries, sometimes producing useful signal, sometimes producing hype.

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), the challenge is to observe this kind of space without amplifying misuse or accepting every claim at face value.

## Current limit

I need to keep separating verified technical behavior from screenshots, claims and community narratives.
