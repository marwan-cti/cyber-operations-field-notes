# Roni Carta supply chain lessons

**Type:** supply chain security / bug bounty mindset / dependency risk

## Why it is on my radar

This note is linked to the Roni Carta profile, but focused on what his work made me understand about supply chain security. I discovered him through interviews, conferences, LinkedIn and articles around Lupin & Holmes / Depi.

## What stuck with me

What I found powerful is the way supply chain attacks change the attacker’s path. Instead of attacking the front door of a company, the attacker can look upstream: dependencies, packages, domains, maintainers, CI/CD, build systems and forgotten trust relationships.

That way of thinking feels very close to CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence): understand where the real leverage is, not only where the obvious vulnerability is.

## What I take from it

Roni Carta’s work helped me understand that supply chain security is not only an AppSec topic. It is also about mapping relationships and asking what an attacker would actually trust, abuse or inherit.

It connects well with my interest in XZ Utils, dependency confusion and the security of AI agent ecosystems.

## Current limit

I still need more hands-on practice with package ecosystems, dependency confusion scenarios and CI/CD security.

Tronche de Tech interviews, Roni Carta’s public posts/articles, Lupin & Holmes / Depi material and supply chain security discussions.
