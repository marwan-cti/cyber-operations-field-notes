# Micode

## Status

Draft v1 — media / cyber culture note.

## How I discovered it

I discovered Micode through YouTube as one of the major French-language entry points into cybersecurity and internet culture.

Micode helped me connect cyber topics with accessible storytelling: scams, hackers, infrastructure, cybercrime, investigations and the way digital systems affect ordinary people.

## Why it matters in my cyber culture

Micode matters because it shows the importance of communication in cybersecurity.

A lot of cyber topics are hard to approach from the outside. Good vulgarization can make a subject visible, understandable and emotionally memorable. For someone building a cyber culture, this kind of content can be the first step before moving toward reports, labs, MITRE ATT&CK, vendor research and technical documentation.

For me, Micode is not a final technical authority. It is a discovery layer: a way to encounter subjects, people and stories that I can later deepen through more rigorous sources.

## Area

- French-language cybersecurity vulgarization.
- Internet investigations.
- Scams and online fraud.
- Hacker culture.
- Tech storytelling.
- Entry-level cyber awareness.

## What I learned from this source

- Cybersecurity communication matters.
- A strong narrative can make complex incidents easier to understand.
- French-language cyber media helped me build curiosity before deeper technical study.
- Popular content should be used as a starting point, not as the evidence base.
- The gap between public storytelling and technical evidence is something an analyst must learn to manage.

## Relevance for CTI

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), Micode is indirectly relevant as a cultural and discovery source.

It helps surface topics and human stories, but CTI work requires going further: identifying primary sources, separating facts from framing, checking dates, mapping TTPs (Tactics, Techniques and Procedures), and evaluating confidence.

This is useful for my learning because CTI is not only about technical knowledge. It is also about explaining complex events clearly without losing rigor.

## Caveats

Micode targets a broad audience. That is valuable, but it means I should not treat the content as a substitute for technical documentation or original research.

When a Micode video introduces a case, my next step should be to look for vendor reports, official advisories, researcher blogs, court documents, conference talks or primary technical material.
