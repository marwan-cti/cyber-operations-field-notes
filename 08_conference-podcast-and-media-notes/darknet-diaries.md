# Darknet Diaries

**Type:** cyber podcast / storytelling / hacker culture

## Why it is on my radar

Darknet Diaries is one of the podcasts that helped build my cyber culture beyond tools and definitions. I discovered it while looking for long-form cyber stories.

## What stuck with me

What I value is the human and operational side of the stories: motivation, mistakes, ego, money, fear, social engineering, insider behavior and real-world consequences.

It made incidents feel less abstract and helped me remember how messy cyber really is.

## What I take from it

Darknet Diaries reinforced my interest in CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), because the podcast shows that understanding an incident often requires linking technique, people, incentives and context.

## Current limit

It is a narrative source, not a technical source. I use it to discover and remember topics, then I need to validate details elsewhere.

## Resources I actually used

Darknet Diaries episodes around hackers, breaches, cybercrime, espionage, crypto, social engineering and incident stories.
