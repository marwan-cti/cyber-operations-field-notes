## Status

Draft v1 — podcast / media ecosystem note.

## How I discovered it

I discovered Underscore_ through the French tech and cybersecurity YouTube ecosystem around Micode.

What makes Underscore_ useful in my learning path is the panel / interview format: it brings practitioners, researchers, entrepreneurs and security people into longer conversations than classic short-form YouTube explainers.

## Why it matters in my cyber culture

Underscore_ matters because it helped expose me to cybersecurity as a living ecosystem, not only as a set of tools.

Through interviews and discussions, the format can connect technical topics with careers, business, research, regulation, incident stories and personal trajectories. That is important for a reconversion profile because understanding the ecosystem helps clarify which roles exist and where my own strengths fit.

## Area

- French-language tech and cybersecurity discussions.
- Practitioner interviews.
- Cybersecurity vulgarization with more depth than short videos.
- AI, hacking, entrepreneurship and digital culture.
- Bridge between mainstream tech culture and specialized cyber topics.

## What I learned from this source

- Cybersecurity is an ecosystem of people, companies, researchers, educators and communities.
- Interviews can reveal how practitioners think, not only what tools they use.
- Long-form discussion helps connect technical subjects with career paths and business context.
- Media sources can point toward researchers, companies and incidents worth deeper study.
- A CTI analyst needs both technical curiosity and ecosystem awareness.

## Relevance for CTI

For CTI (Cyber Threat Intelligence), Underscore_ is useful as a discovery and ecosystem source.

It can help identify relevant people, emerging themes, security companies, incident narratives and debates. But it should be followed by stronger sources when producing analysis: vendor reports, official advisories, technical blogs, conference slides and primary documentation.

## Caveats

Underscore_ is media content, not a formal intelligence source. The format may simplify, dramatize or prioritize accessible discussion over technical completeness.

The right analytical posture is to use it to discover topics and then validate claims through more technical sources.
